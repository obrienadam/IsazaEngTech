Advanced Mathematical Modelling

One of our core services includes the advanced mathematical modelling of real-world engineering applications. We use a diverse array of techniques to model phenomena including:

  1. Heat transfer and thermal systems
    • Moving bed heat exchangers, pebble bed nuclear reactors, double-pipe and shell-and-tube heat exchangers, air coolers, etc.
    • HVAC design and optimization
    • Heat conduction in solids of different geometries
  2. Fluid systems
    • Incompressible and compressible pipe flow, aerodynamics and moving boundary problems
    • Network optimization of multicomponent systems (i.e. industrial process modeling)
    • Mutiphase systems – combustion, bitumen extraction, sedimentation, etc.

Our Advantage

Our team consists of highly trained engineers with backgrounds in both industry and academics. We seek to bridge the gap between novel, abstract ideas in academia and practical implementation in industrial settings.

Analytical Mathematics

Our team has experience in conducting cutting edge mathematical research in Partial Differential Equations (PDEs). We specialize in novel approaches for deriving governing equations, such as the volume averaging method. Our mathematical techniques for solving these problems include advanced integral transforms, Green’s functions and separation of variables. This provides us with a deeper insight into the underlying physics of many engineering problems.

Numerical Mathematics

Our team also has extensive experience in implementing and developing state-of- the-art numerical modelling software for solving complex PDEs. Our expertise includes the development of Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) software using the Finite-Volume Method (FVM), numerical optimization, and Finite-Difference methods. We’ve successfully explored problems ranging from the combustion of laboratory-scale burners to the dynamics of liquid droplets. We also have considerable experience in High Performance Computing (HPC) for resource intensive problems.
