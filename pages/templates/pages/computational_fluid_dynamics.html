Computational Fluid Dynamics

Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) is a rapidly growing field for modelling fluid systems. Significant increases in available computing power have enabled engineers to use numerical models to solve complex fluid equations. This allows them to model everything from the aerodynamics of vehicles to combustion within gas turbine engines. Our team currently focuses our CFD efforts in the modelling of fluid components in manufacturing sysytems.

CFD of Modelling of Fluid System Components

Accurately describing/predicting the performance of large fluid systems also requires understanding the source of inefficiencies at the component levels. Approximately 20–30% of all energy in fluid systems is consumed by minor losses. Our CFD efforts seek both to reduce the source of these losses by making adjustments to the geometries of these components. Additionally, we're using CFD to create a more complete description of losses, in order to obtain more reliable results from our flagship node network fluid system modeller Poiseulle.
